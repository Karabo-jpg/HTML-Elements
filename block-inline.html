Block vs Inline Elements

Music Theme

In the world of music composition, understanding rhythm and harmony is absolutely essential for any aspiring musician. These elements form the foundation of every melody you hear.

The Symphony Orchestra (Block Element: Div)

This orchestra section demonstrates block-level behavior. The purple border shows that it spans the full width of the stage (parent container). Like a full orchestra setup, divs take up all available space to organize instruments (content) effectively.

Music Genres (Block Element: Section)

This genre section also demonstrates block-level behavior. The orange border illustrates that sections, like divs, are full-width block elements. Examples of genres include Jazz, Classical, Rock, and Hip-Hop, each deserving its own dedicated section in a music library.

Key Difference Summary:
Block elements (div, section) = Full width, new line before and after
Inline elements (strong, span) = Content width only, flows with text

Here are two inline elements side by side: Sheet Music and Metronome - they flow together on the musician's desk without breaking to new lines!
